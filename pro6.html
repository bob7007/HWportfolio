Second Order low-pass Active Butterworth Filter



Previous Next


The Low Pass Butterworth Filter shown in Figure 4a is a second order filter that allows low frequency components to proceed to the output while attenuating higher frequency components.

The filter is a second order filter, meaning that there is a rather steep drop off in output at the frequency where the filter attenuation begins. For this experiment the filter is made with an operational amplifier at its heart.

The frequency where the filter begins attenuation (recognized as frequency at which the output drops by 3 decibels or -3dB below the filters low frequency gain) and the amount of gain (K) are determined by the values of associated resistors and capacitors in the feedback and input circuits.

Some applications for the filter include: anti-aliasing for data converters, radars, high quality audio and motion analysis.
