Complete Complementary Symmetry Audio Amplifier



Previous Next


Using a small input A.C signal to obtain a large power gain. The two-stage amplifier is created by a combination of a class AB current amplifier and a class A common emitter voltage amplifier.

After doing harmonic analysis of spectral lines as well as Input Signal Voltage Amplitude Response it was concluded that most of the energy was concentrated at 1khz.

Testing:

AC signal set to 80mV at 1KHz produces a gain of ≈124.5

AC signal set to 10mV at 1KHz produces a gain of ≈175.5

AC signal set to 20mV at 1KHz produces a gain of ≈170.2

AC signal set to 50mV at 1KHz produces a gain of ≈158

Conclusion: Most amplification gain is obtained at 10Mv at 1KHz frequency.
